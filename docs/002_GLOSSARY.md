# LeadForgeAI — Glossary

Version: 1.0.0

Status: Draft

---

# Purpose

This document defines the meaning of every important word used throughout the project.

Every AI, developer and contributor must use these definitions consistently.

---

# Business Lead

A business entity collected from public sources.

Example:

- Restaurant
- Hospital
- School
- Gym
- Printing Press
- Law Firm

---

# People Lead

A publicly identifiable professional associated with a business.

Examples:

- Founder
- CEO
- Director
- Dentist
- Doctor
- Lawyer
- Marketing Manager

Only publicly available professional information should be stored.

---

# Lead

A Lead is either:

- Business Lead

or

- People Lead

---

# Enrichment

The process of adding more information to an existing lead.

Example:

Business Name

↓

Website

↓

Email

↓

Social Media

↓

AI Analysis

---

# Crawl

Opening a website and collecting publicly available information.

---

# Scrape

Collecting publicly available information from supported sources.

---

# Source

A platform from which data is collected.

Examples:

Google Maps

Business Website

Public Company Profile

Public Social Profile

---

# Workspace

A local LeadForgeAI project created by the user.

One workspace contains:

Database

Settings

Campaigns

Templates

History

---

# Campaign

A collection of outreach activities.

Example:

Dentists in Patna

↓

449 Emails

↓

Tracking

↓

Reports

---

# Workflow

A sequence of automated actions executed by n8n.

---

# Module

An independent part of LeadForgeAI.

Examples:

Maps

Website

Email

AI

Campaign

Settings

---

# AI Analysis

Business intelligence generated from collected public information.

Example:

Business Summary

Pain Points

Suggested Service

Lead Score

Personalized Opening Line

---

# Health Check

Automatic verification that every required local service is running.

Example:

Docker

n8n

Database

Backend

AI

---

# Local Mode

Everything runs on the user's own computer.

---

# Self Hosted

The software is fully controlled by the user.

No cloud account is required.

---

# Public Data

Information intentionally published by businesses or professionals for public access.

Examples include:

Business name

Business phone number

Company website

Public contact email

Business address

Opening hours

Public social media profiles

The software must not attempt to collect private or restricted information.
